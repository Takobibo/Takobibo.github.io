Biography
Growth & Achievements
Weijie Huang grew up in a strict family where his parents demanded he become a doctor. But he secretly loved nature—watching seeds sprout, bees dance, and fungi network underground. After earning dual doctorates in AI and Biomedical Science from MIT, he felt hollow inside. So he disappeared for two years, traveling to remote villages and rainforests, studying how traditional foods affect human emotion. When he returned, he founded SUPERGO with a mission: create food that heals the spirit, not just the body.
His breakthrough came when he merged AI with food science. Using genomic sequencing and molecular modeling, SUPERGO developed “Emotion-Adaptive Nutrition”—products like “Anxiety Fizz” that help calm users within minutes. The market loved it. Within three years, SUPERGO became a global brand. Today, SUPERGO has research outposts on the Moon and Mars, growing optimized crops in biodomes. Their products are standard on space flights and beloved by extraterrestrial colonists. From Tokyo to Titan, SUPERGO proves that science can serve the human heart.
Future Vision
SUPERGO’s future has three pillars. First, “Bio-Planetary Integration”: using CRISPR to grow crops on alien soil. Second, “Emotional AI Sommeliers”: wearables that monitor your hormones and suggest the perfect product in real time. Third, “The Open Pantry Initiative”: an interplanetary open-source platform for recipes and emotional feedback.
Beyond technology, Dr. Huang dreams of ending food scarcity and nutritional injustice. He wants SUPERGO to heal the broken relationship between humanity and nature. “My parents wanted me to fix people one at a time,” he reflects. “Now, I want to fix the way people eat—and in doing so, fix something much larger.” In the next decade, he plans to launch a self-sustaining SUPERGO farm on Mars—a gift to future generations, and proof that even from an unhappy childhood, something beautiful can grow.
